Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

When I'm working on a project, I think a lot about the message I want to convey, the tools I use, and doing the right thing. I believe it's important to communicate clearly, use the best tech available, and always consider the impact of my work. This way, I ensure that my designs are not just attractive but also meaningful and responsible.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

When I design, I focus on making things both good-looking and functional. I think about how different colors and fonts will make my design pop and be easy for everyone to read. I want to ensure that everyone, even people with disabilities, can enjoy what I create.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I find that working with my classmates brings out the best in my designs. When we all pitch in with our ideas, I discover new and better ways to approach my work. It's like getting advice and insights from many different people!



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

I believe that improvement comes from making changes and trying new things. Whether I'm sketching on paper, designing on a computer, or making a video, I like to experiment with colors, fonts, and images to see what feels right.

5. Learn to present your work.

After putting in the effort, I want to showcase my work the best way I can. This means explaining why I made certain decisions and how I think my design will make a difference. Choosing the right colors and fonts is key to making my presentation clear and engaging.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

In my class where I learned about writing and design, I picked up some really important stuff about how to communicate well, use technology, and think about ethics in my designs. First off, I got better at using language and visuals to tell a story about my designs, making sure my ideas were clear and catchy. Then, there was the tech part. Learning some coding skills opened up a whole new world for me, especially in web and digital design. It's amazing how much you can do with the right tech tools to create and show off your designs. Lastly, the ethics side of things really made me think. I learned to be mindful of how my designs affect people and the planet. This meant making sure my designs were fair, easy for everyone to use, and didn't harm the environment. It was all about bringing together good communication, cool tech, and thoughtful design to make something that really matters.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Applying design principles like balance, contrast, and unity was crucial in my work. These principles guided me to create designs that were not only aesthetically pleasing but also functionally effective. I learned how to make my designs more coherent, user-friendly, and visually appealing.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Engaging in a workshop environment was transformative for me. Collaborating with peers, exchanging ideas, and receiving feedback enriched my learning experience. Facilitating a research workshop particularly enhanced my leadership and communication skills, vital for collaborative design processes.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

I practiced revising my designs across different media forms, which helped me understand the unique demands of each medium. This practice was essential for developing my versatility as a designer and taught me how different strategies are required for digital, print, or interactive media.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presenting my work effectively was as crucial as the design process itself. I honed my skills in articulating my ideas clearly, showcasing my designs engagingly, and responding constructively to feedback. These presentation skills were key in persuading and impressing stakeholders, clients, or peers about the effectiveness of my designs.
