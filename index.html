Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

When I'm working on a project, I think a lot about the message I want to convey, the tools I use, and doing the right thing. I believe it's important to communicate clearly, use the best tech available, and always consider the impact of my work. This way, I ensure that my designs are not just attractive but also meaningful and responsible.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

When I design, I focus on making things both good-looking and functional. I think about how different colors and fonts will make my design pop and be easy for everyone to read. I want to ensure that everyone, even people with disabilities, can enjoy what I create.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I find that working with my classmates brings out the best in my designs. When we all pitch in with our ideas, I discover new and better ways to approach my work. It's like getting advice and insights from many different people!



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

I believe that improvement comes from making changes and trying new things. Whether I'm sketching on paper, designing on a computer, or making a video, I like to experiment with colors, fonts, and images to see what feels right.

5. Learn to present your work.

After putting in the effort, I want to showcase my work the best way I can. This means explaining why I made certain decisions and how I think my design will make a difference. Choosing the right colors and fonts is key to making my presentation clear and engaging.
